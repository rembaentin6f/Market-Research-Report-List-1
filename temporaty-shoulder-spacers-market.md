<p><p>The temporary shoulder spacers market is categorized into cementless and cemented types. Cementless spacers utilize a porous surface that promotes biological fixation without the need for cement, allowing for easier removal and potential for better bone integration. In contrast, cemented spacers rely on acrylic cement for stability, providing immediate fixation and load-bearing capacity. Both types are designed for short-term use in patients needing temporary joint stabilization during infection treatment or prior to a definitive surgical procedure.</p></p>
