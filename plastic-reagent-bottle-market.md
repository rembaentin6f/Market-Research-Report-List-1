<p><p>The plastic reagent bottle market comprises various types, each suited for specific applications. Polystyrene (PS) bottles are lightweight and transparent, ideal for storing non-hazardous reagents. Polyethylene (PE) bottles offer excellent chemical resistance and durability for various laboratory uses. Polycarbonate (PC) bottles are known for their strength and clarity, suitable for more demanding applications. Polypropylene (PP) bottles provide high chemical resistance and are often used for storage of aggressive reagents. PETG bottles combine clarity with chemical resistance, while "Others" include alternatives like acrylic or fluorinated bottles for specialized requirements.</p></p>
