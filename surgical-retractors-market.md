<p><p>The surgical retractors market encompasses two main types: hand-held retractors and self-retaining retractors. Hand-held retractors require manual holding by the surgeon or an assistant, offering flexibility during procedures. They are commonly used in surgeries where the surgeon can maintain control. Self-retaining retractors, on the other hand, are designed to hold incisions open without continuous manual support, allowing surgeons to focus on the operation. Both types are crucial for improving visibility and access during various surgical procedures.</p></p>
