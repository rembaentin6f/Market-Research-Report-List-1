<p><p>The small signal general purpose transistor market is primarily divided into two types: bipolar and unipolar transistors. Bipolar transistors, or BJTs, utilize both electron and hole charge carriers, making them suitable for applications requiring high current gain and amplification. Unipolar transistors, such as MOSFETs, rely on a single type of charge carrier, offering advantages like faster switching speeds and higher input impedance. Both types find extensive use in signal processing, amplification, and switching applications across diverse industries.</p></p>
