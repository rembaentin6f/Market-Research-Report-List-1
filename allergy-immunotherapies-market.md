<p><p>The allergy immunotherapy market is primarily divided into two types: subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT). SCIT involves administering allergens via injections, gradually increasing doses to desensitize the immune system, effectively treating severe allergic reactions over time. Conversely, SLIT employs allergen tablets or drops placed under the tongue, offering a more convenient, non-invasive alternative with fewer side effects. Both approaches aim to improve patient quality of life by reducing allergy symptoms and long-term reliance on medications.</p></p>
