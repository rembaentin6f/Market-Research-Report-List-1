<p><p>Nickel powder for MLCC (Multi-layer Ceramic Capacitors) electrodes is primarily produced using two advanced methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD). The CVD method involves the chemical reaction of gaseous precursors to deposit nickel onto substrates, offering uniformity and precision in particle size. In contrast, PVD utilizes physical means, such as sputtering or evaporation, to deposit nickel, resulting in high-density powders. Both methods cater to the specific demands of the MLCC industry, impacting performance and efficiency.</p></p>
