<p><p>The allergic conjunctivitis market consists of several types based on triggers and duration. Seasonal allergic conjunctivitis occurs during specific seasons due to pollen or molds. Perennial allergic conjunctivitis is triggered year-round by allergens like dust mites or pet dander. Giant papillary conjunctivitis is linked to contact lens use, characterized by large papillae on the eyelid. Vernal keratoconjunctivitis occurs primarily in warmer climates and commonly affects young males. Atopic keratoconjunctivitis is associated with atopic dermatitis, presenting chronic symptoms impacting vision.</p></p>
