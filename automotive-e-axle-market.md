<p><p>The automotive e-axle market is primarily categorized into front e-axles and rear e-axles. Front e-axles are integrated into the front axle of a vehicle, enhancing traction and driving dynamics, especially in electric vehicles (EVs). They are vital for applications requiring precise control and torque distribution. Rear e-axles, conversely, are located at the rear, providing power primarily for rear-wheel drive configurations. This arrangement supports better weight distribution, stability, and acceleration, catering to both performance-oriented and utility vehicles.</p></p>
