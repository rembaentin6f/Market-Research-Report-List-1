<p><p>The automotive carbon brake rotors market is primarily divided into two types: carbon-carbon matrix and carbon-ceramic matrix. Carbon-carbon matrix rotors are made of a composite material designed for high-performance applications, offering excellent heat resistance and lightweight properties, making them ideal for racing and high-end sports cars. On the other hand, carbon-ceramic matrix rotors combine carbon fibers with a ceramic matrix, providing superior braking performance, reduced wear, and improved heat dissipation, suitable for luxury vehicles and high-performance applications.</p></p>
