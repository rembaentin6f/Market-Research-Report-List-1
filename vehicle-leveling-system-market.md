<p><p>The vehicle leveling system market comprises two primary types: electric and hydraulic leveling systems. Electric leveling systems use electronically controlled actuators to adjust the vehicle's height, offering precise control and faster response times. In contrast, hydraulic leveling systems rely on fluid-filled cylinders to raise or lower the vehicle, providing powerful support and stability, particularly in heavier vehicles. Both systems are designed to enhance passenger comfort and improve handling by maintaining a consistent ride height under varying loads.</p></p>
